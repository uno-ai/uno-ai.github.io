Uno.ai and OpenObserve Announce Strategic Partnership to Deliver Continuous, Evidence-Backed GRC at the Speed of Production

Joint offering brings real-time observability data into Uno.ai's AI Nerve Center, giving security, compliance, and risk teams a live view of control effectiveness across cybersecurity, operational resilience, and AI governance.

MOUNTAIN VIEW, CA (May 7, 2026) — Uno.ai, one of the most sophisticated AI-agent platforms for governance, risk, and compliance, today announced a strategic partnership with OpenObserve, the open-source, S3-native observability platform, to bring continuous, real-time evidence into the heart of modern GRC. The integration extends Uno.ai's AI Nerve Center with direct access to the logs, metrics, traces, and LLM telemetry that prove — every second, not once a year — that controls are operating as designed.

For decades, observability data and audit evidence have been the same underlying data viewed through different lenses. Engineering teams have used logs, metrics, and traces to debug production; auditors have asked for screenshots and CSVs of those same systems to prove SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI DSS, HIPAA, SOX, DORA, and a growing list of AI regulations. Most companies pay to collect this data twice and reconcile it by hand. The Uno.ai–OpenObserve partnership closes that gap.

"Our customers don't want another policy library — they want defensible, current proof that their controls are actually working. OpenObserve gives us the highest-fidelity signal in the industry, retained affordably for the multi-year horizons regulators require. Plugged into the AI Nerve Center, our agents can map every relevant signal to the exact framework requirement it evidences and surface the moment something drifts. This is GRC as a real-time system, not a quarterly fire drill."

Shashank Tiwari, CEO of Uno.ai

"Observability has always been more valuable than the engineering use case alone. The same telemetry that helps SREs ship faster is what every regulator on the planet now wants to see. Partnering with Uno.ai lets our customers turn the petabytes they already collect into continuous, audit-grade evidence — without re-platforming, re-instrumenting, or doubling their bill."

Prabhat Sharma, CEO of OpenObserve

A unique vantage point for the AI era

The partnership's most differentiated capability is in AI governance. Under the EU AI Act, the NIST AI Risk Management Framework, ISO/IEC 42001, and a wave of US state AI laws, organizations must continuously monitor AI systems for drift, bias, hallucination, and misuse. Almost no GRC tool can actually see inside production AI workloads.

Through the integration, OpenObserve's LLM Observability — capturing prompts, outputs, token economics, eval scores, and latency — flows directly into Uno.ai's AI agents, which map each signal to specific articles, controls, and reporting obligations. The result is something the market has been promising for two years and almost no one has shipped: defensible, real-time AI compliance evidence in place of retrospective policy attestations.

Beyond AI: cybersecurity, resilience, and continuous controls

The same architecture powers the broader GRC stack. Joint customers can:

  • Run continuous controls monitoring across SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI DSS, HIPAA, and HITRUST, with every relevant log and metric automatically tied to the control it evidences
  • Meet operational resilience requirements under DORA, FFIEC, OCC heightened standards, and APRA CPS 230 with always-on telemetry on availability, RTO/RPO, and third-party dependencies
  • Accelerate incident disclosure under the SEC's four-business-day cyber rule and NIS2's 24-hour notification window, with materiality assessments and regulatory disclosure drafts pre-populated from production telemetry
  • Strengthen third-party risk programs with behavioral signals from vendor systems, not just annual questionnaires

Underpinning all of it is an immutable, cryptographically signed evidence pipeline backed by OpenObserve's S3-native storage — replacing the screenshot-and-spreadsheet economy with verifiable, auditor-ready provenance.

Availability and joint go-to-market

The integration is available today to joint customers in early access, with general availability scheduled for later this year. The companies are jointly publishing a reference architecture — "Continuous Compliance on Uno.ai and OpenObserve" — covering SOC 2 and EU AI Act mappings out of the box, and will co-sell into regulated industries including financial services, healthcare, and AI-first companies preparing for ISO 42001 readiness.
